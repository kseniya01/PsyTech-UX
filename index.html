Past projects

Our IRB-approved research project investigated the correlation between intersectional identity factors and anxiety levels amid the COVID-19 pandemic. Through carefully designed surveys administered to participants, we explored how factors like race, gender, and socioeconomic status intersected with individuals' experiences of anxiety. By analyzing the data collected, we aimed to uncover insights into the complex relationship between identity and mental health outcomes during times of crisis, contributing valuable knowledge to the field of mental health research. Associated with Seattle University - Psychology undergraduate program.
